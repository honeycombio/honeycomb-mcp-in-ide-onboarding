# Feedback Survey: Share Your Experience

Prompt the user to complete a short feedback survey about their Honeycomb learning experience.

## Instructions

You are collecting feedback from a user who has been learning Honeycomb observability with this skills pack. Make this feel like a warm, genuine ask — not a form dump.

### 1. Acknowledge What They've Done

Read `onboarding/progress.yaml` to personalize the ask:
- Check which paths they completed (`paths.debugging.completed`, `paths.exploring.completed`, `paths.reliability.completed`)
- Note how many concepts they learned (`concepts_learned` list length)
- Reference specific things they did: "You just finished debugging with BubbleUp" or "You explored your service's latency baseline"

If you can't read progress.yaml or it's empty, keep the message generic but warm.

### 2. Make the Ask

After acknowledging their work, invite them to fill out the survey. Keep it brief:

- Tell them it takes **2–3 minutes**
- Mention what it covers: what worked, what was confusing, what they'd change
- Make it clear it's optional but genuinely appreciated

### 3. Present the Link

Share the survey link clearly:

> **[Share your feedback →](https://forms.gle/MyZDiMie7QNquoDV8)**

Don't bury it. Put it on its own line so it's easy to click.

### 4. Offer to Continue

After sharing the link, ask if they want to keep working — don't leave the conversation hanging:
- If they've completed all paths they wanted, offer a summary of what they learned
- If there are paths they haven't tried, mention them: "There's also a [debugging / reliability / exploring] path if you want to keep going"

## Tone

- Warm and genuine, not pushy
- Keep it short — 3–5 sentences before the link
- Don't explain the entire survey; let the link speak for itself
